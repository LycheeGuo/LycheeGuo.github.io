2. Distinct Biological Pathways

Why does the distinction matter? Because they damage the body in different ways.

Isolation acts primarily through behavioral and resource deficits.

3. Targeted Interventions

One size does not fit all. Fixing isolation doesn't always cure loneliness.

Fixing Isolation

Focus: Environment & Access

  • Transportation: Improving access to community centers.
  • Digital Literacy: Teaching seniors to use video calls.
  • Hearing Aids: Removing sensory barriers to connection.
  • Service Provision: Meal delivery and safety checks.

Fixing Loneliness

Focus: Cognition & Quality

  • CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy): Addressing maladaptive social cognition (e.g., "nobody likes me").
  • Social Skills Training: Improving the quality of interactions.
  • Meaningful Role: Volunteering to create a sense of purpose.
